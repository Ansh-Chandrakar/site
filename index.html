About Me

I'm a curious and motivated physics student with a strong interest in science and technology. For as long as I can remember, I've always been fascinated by astronomy and astrophysics. That drive to understand how the universe ticks at a fundamental level led me to pursue my MSc in Physics at IIT Hyderabad.


While the cosmos looks outward, I am equally captivated by the digital world. I love computers and am actively pushing myself to learn more about them. I spend a lot of my time bridging the gap between abstract physics and computational reality, branching out from my usual Python scripts and physics simulations into areas like web development and data analysis. I'm fascinated by how computational thinking applies to everyday decisions, which is why Algorithms to Live By by Brian Christian, Thomas L. Griffiths, and Tom Griffiths is one of my all-time favorite books.


Beyond the Terminal

Life isn't just about equations and algorithms. When I need to clear my head, you'll usually find me out cruising the roads on my bike, taking in the world at my own pace, or looking into performance tweaks for my car. I'm an avid photographer, always looking to capture unique perspectives.


When I'm unwinding at home, I spend a lot of time listening to music—especially trying to nail down guitar riffs on my own guitar. I also love playing video games, and catching up on movies and series. This site is a space to capture all of these intersecting passions.

Ongoing Work

A brief look at the problems and projects I am currently exploring.

Demystifying Quantum Entanglement

Ongoing Manuscript

Developing a pedagogical article aimed at making quantum entanglement more accessible to a broader physics audience. The core of the work relies on Python and QuTiP to simulate systems of coupled quantum harmonic oscillators. By visually mapping out concepts like Von Neumann entropy, entanglement dynamics, and decoherence, the goal is to bridge the gap between abstract quantum theory and intuitive computational models.

Galactic Cosmic Ray Propagation

Computational Physics Group Project

A newly initiated numerical modeling project focusing on the propagation of cosmic rays through the galaxy. We are currently in the early stages of setting up the computational framework to simulate how these high-energy particles diffuse and interact with galactic magnetic fields and the interstellar medium. It's a great exercise in applying numerical methods to large-scale astrophysical phenomena.
